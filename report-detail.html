AI Pattern Analysis

This report describes a platform-switching social engineering attack — a deliberate, premeditated fraud tactic commonly used by Nigerian online scammers. The fraudster steers victims away from reversible payment platforms (Opay, PalmPay) to less reversible ones (Moniepoint, direct bank transfer) by fabricating technical problems. This is not coincidental — it is a calculated move to reduce the victim's ability to dispute or reverse the transaction.

⚠️

Red Flag: Any vendor who asks you to switch payment apps for "network reasons" is almost certainly a scammer. Legitimate sellers accept payment on whatever platform you prefer.

⚠️

Red Flag: Sudden disappearance after payment — blocking on all platforms simultaneously — indicates the vendor prepared for this exit. It was never a legitimate transaction.

🛡️

How to stay safe: Always insist on using a payment platform with buyer dispute protection. Never pay someone who refuses your preferred platform. Verify accounts here before sending money.

What happened

I found a Yamaha turntable listed for ₦65,000 on Instagram. The seller seemed legitimate — they had product photos, voice notes, and previous "buyer testimonials" in their story highlights. When I was ready to pay, I tried to send via Opay but they said there was a network issue on their end and asked me to use Moniepoint instead. After I sent the money and confirmed, they immediately blocked me on Instagram and WhatsApp. Their account is now deactivated.
